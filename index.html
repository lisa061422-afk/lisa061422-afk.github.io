Ph.D. Candidate · George Mason University

Renke Wang

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at George Mason University. My research focuses on scheduling, resource allocation, and control co-design for cyber-physical systems (CPS), with the goal of optimizing system-level performance while respecting resource limits, safety requirements, and system dynamics. I develop online model predictive control (MPC) frameworks and optimization formulations for real-time scheduling and control, with applications to warehouse robot coordination, traffic vehicle scheduling, networked control systems, and human-robot collaboration.

Projects

Scheduling and Control Co-Design with Determined Resource Occupation Orders

This project studies systems whose resource-occupation sequences are determined in advance. The goal is to jointly design scheduling decisions and control actions so robots or vehicles can move through shared resources safely and efficiently while satisfying resource, safety, timing, and system-dynamics constraints.

Single-Zone Shared-Resource Scheduling and Control

Contention-resolving MPC for coordinating robots or vehicles through localized shared-resource areas, such as warehouse merging zones or traffic intersections.

Networked Multi-Zone Scheduling and Control

Contention-resolving MPC for robots or vehicles moving through multiple coupled shared-resource zones in warehouse or traffic networks, including centralized formulations and distributed solution frameworks for larger resource graphs.

Resource Allocation with Scheduling and Control Joint Design

This project studies scheduling and control problems where each system’s recurring tasks may select among multiple available resources. Unlike the determined-order setting, the resource occupation sequence is not fixed in advance and must be decided jointly with scheduling and control.

Human Attention Allocation in Multi-Human Multi-Robot (MHMR) Collaborative Systems

Scheduling and allocation of limited human attention in MHMR collaborative systems, where multiple robots may request assistance from human operators.

Robust Co-Design for Blimp Motion Control with Heterogeneous Computational Resources

Scheduling and control co-design for multiple blimps sharing limited computational resources with different working efficiencies.

Path Planning and Scheduling Co-Design for Multi-Merging Zones (Ongoing Work)

Joint path planning and scheduling for controlled systems moving through multiple merging zones, using learning-guided methods to support path-selection and resource-access decisions.

Robust Control Design under Timing Perturbations

This project studies control systems where the actual control-update time may deviate from the estimated or scheduled update time. The focus is on maintaining stability and performance when timing perturbations affect piecewise-constant control signals.

Linear Time-Invariant Systems under Timing Perturbations

Tube-based MPC with dynamic feedback gain for linear time-invariant networked control systems affected by random timing perturbations.

Nonlinear Systems under Timing Perturbations (Ongoing Work)

Extension of the timing-perturbation framework to nonlinear systems using Jacobian linearization and Koopman-based approximation methods.
